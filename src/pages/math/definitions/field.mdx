# Definition

A field $$ \mathbb{F} $$ is a set with the following operations:

1. **Addition:** $$ +: \mathbb{F}\times \mathbb{F}\rightarrow \mathbb{F} $$
1. **Multiplication:** $$ \cdot \text{ or } \times: \mathbb{F}\times \mathbb{F}\rightarrow \mathbb{F} $$

which satisfies the following axioms:

1. **Commutativity:** $$\forall x,y\in\mathbb{F}[x+y=y+x]$$
1. **Associativity:** $$\forall x,y,z\in\mathbb{F}[(x+y)+z=x+(y+z)]$$
1. **Additive Identity:** $$\exists 0\in\mathbb{F}[\forall x\in\mathbb{F}[x + 0 = 0]]$$
1. **Multiplicative Identity:** $$\exists 1\in\mathbb{F}[\forall x\in\mathbb{F}[1\times x = x]]$$
1. **Additive Inverse:** $$\forall x\in\mathbb{F}[\exists -x\in\mathbb{F}[x + (-x) = 0]]$$
1. **Multiplicative Inverse:** $$\forall x\in\mathbb{F}[x\ne 0\rightarrow \exists x^{-1}\in\mathbb{F}[x\times (x^{-1})=1]]$$
1. **Distributivity:** $$\forall x,y,z\in\mathbb{F}[x\times(y+z)=(x\times y) + (x\times z)]$$

## Prime Field

A prime field $$\mathbb{F}_p$$ of characteristic $$ p $$ where $$ p $$ is a prime number is the set of integers modulo $$ p $$.

It may also be stated as the set of equivalences classes of the integers under the equivalence relation of congruence modulo $$ p $$.

> $$ \{[0],[1],[2],\ldots,[p-1]\} $$

# Non-Examples

## Integers
The integers $$ \mathbb{Z} $$ are not a field. They satisfy all properties except the existence of a multiplicative inverse for all nonzero elements, which makes it a [ring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integer).

Take, for example, the number 3. $$ \mathbb{Z} $$ does not contain $$ \tfrac{1}{3} $$, so 3 does not have a multiplicative inverse in $$ \mathbb{Z} $$.

## Quaternions

Quaternions are not necessarily commutative. By definition, $$ i\times j = k$$ but $$ j \times i = -k$$ so $$ i \times j \ne j \times i$$
